The Animation
The first time we were introduced to coding in the Arduino IDE, I understood that whatever was to be displayed on the screen would be in a loop. This made me want to think about what things go in a loop: instantly, my brain went to carousels at carnivals. I am personally very fond of carousels and carnivals, so I stuck with it. I created a PNG pixel art of a carousel horse and made it into a Sprite. Once I could make it go around the screen, I made it go up and down to imitate a horse on a carousel. I added two more horses to fill up the screen for a total of three horses in the loop.

I wanted to incorporate a random element to my animation, so I displayed the words "HOP ON!" for 5 seconds, followed by the carousel horse loop playing for a random time between 5-10 seconds.
The Components and Setup
The following items were used to create the piece:
  • ESP32 with TTGO T-display
  • 220 mAH battery
  • Wires, crimp connecter
  • Heat wrap
  • Cardboard envelope with a frontal cutout
  • Popsicle stick
  • String
The code for the animation was developed in the Arduino IDE, and then uploaded to the ESP32's flash drive via a USB-C connection.

Separately, 220mAH was soldered. Crimp connectors and heat wrap were used to create a connection from the battery to the ESP32. Once the ESP32 was properly powered and attached to the battery, both components were taped to the back of the cardboard envelope, such that the display was visible out of the cutout in its front.

Afterwards, the envelope was decorated with a red pen and hole punched. A piece of string was tied to a popsicle stick and attached through the hole punch in the envelope.

Using a ladder, the popsicle was inserted onto the ceiling vents. Since this installation included other artists, the envelope was dangling from the ceiling alongside many other envelopes and animations. The Artistic Concept
Since the displays are dangling from a string, they are all spinning. I had seen this happen with the prior year's installation and wished to incorporate that into my idea: not only do the horses go round and round in a loop, but the carousel itself is spinning, as they do in real life.
I find carousels to be very reminiscent of my childhood, and something my mom loved to take us to when I was younger. One of the last times she came and visited New York we went on a carousel ride at Central Park. This made me want to incorporate a childish aspect to my design, which came across in my envelope drawing. Since I used a pen, it gives the appearance of a crayon drawing done by a kid.
